Banyanbrain Case Study

Overview:

    Banyanbrain is a digital marketing company. The aim of the company is to help businesses to embark on a successful digital marketing journey while enhancing their customer experience, improving operational efficiency, and increasing sales and revenue.

    Banyanbrain’ website was functional, but not very user friendly. It was full of bugs, stylistically outdated, and inconsistent. I aimed to simplify and beautify the design, while improving the user flow of most tasks. As a small business owner, we wanted to refresh the look and improve the website without breaking the bank. I assured the design was modular, simple, and easy for us to handle without constant assistance of a developer. The designs are fairly basic and unembellished, yet elegant and inviting.

Deliverables:

Interaction Design:

    High-fidelity interactive prototypes for key tasks on Android, iOS and PCs.

UX/UI Design:

Research

    My research first centred around the competition space in order to understand how other web apps are addressing similar issues. More than 80 user survey responses would help quantify what customers are paying for, and what they’re hoping to resolve. And lastly, one-on-one interviews would help me understand users’ journeys and specific issues the app would need to address.

Getting Closer to User-Centred Design

    Superficially it might seem that everyone is interested in the same thing: saving money and time but upon closer inspection the user research made clear that there were divergent motivations.

    Creating personas helped bring some clarity to those divergences, which would become important reference points as functions developed.

    As research and design proceeded, I focused primarily on two personas because they represented heavy emphasis on two key functions: scrutinising usage reports and data to make contact decisions; and reliance on the web app’s service, case study, and portfolio for user choices.
